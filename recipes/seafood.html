Some people add carrots, potatoes and other vegetables to the stew, but I stuck to some simple celery and a bell pepper for mine. Also, if you have caraway seeds you can add some crushed, but I didn’t have any at home so I substitued with cumin instead.

Goulash is traditionally served over small egg noodles or in other parts of the world over mashed potatoes or polenta. Ive always eaten this flavorful stew over rice…but not just any rice…Uncle Ben’s Rice. But however you choose to eat it…its a dish youll love for sure.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs stew meat
  • 5 cloves of Garlic
  • 1 large Onion
  • 1 Red Bell Pepper
  • 2 Bay Leaves
  • 4 tbsp butter
  • 2 tbsp oil
  • Sour cream (for garnish)
  • 1/3 Cup Flour
  • 4 Stalks of Celery

Steps

  1. Start by finely mincing the garlic cloves
  2. Roughly chop the onion, celery and bell pepperin a big dice
  3. In a little bowl, ix together 1/3 cup of flour and a generous teaspoon of paprika
  4. Dust the beef stew chunks with the flour and paprika mix and toss it so that all the pieces are well coated
  5. In a heavy bottom pot or dutch oven, melt 2 tablespoons of butter on medium heat
  6. Ass about a tablespoon of oil to the butter, to keep it from burning
  7. Fry the meat pieces in small batches on high heat and brown them on all sides
  8. Then scoop out the beef and set it aside on a plate
  9. Repea until all the beef is browned
  10. Once all the meat is brownd, add some more butter/oil to the same pot
  11. Toss in the minced garlic and diced onion and sautee
